Neonicotinoids, a class of neuro-active insecticides chemically similar to nicotine, were developed in the 1980s and 90s. Compared to organophosphate and carbamate insecticides, neonicotinoids cause less toxicity in birds and mammals than insects.

Neonicotinoid use has been linked in a range of studies to adverse ecological effects, including honey-bee colony collapse disorder (CCD) and loss of birds due to a reduction in insect populations; the findings used to be conflicting and thus controversial, but recent studies by the EFSA have confirmed the risk to bees. In 2013, the European Union and a few non EU countries restricted the use of certain neonicotinoids; Several states in the United States have also restricted usage of neonicotinoids.
